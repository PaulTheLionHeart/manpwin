Marcus Fractals

Overview

These fractals are contributions to ManpWIN from Marcus Rezende, an early pioneer in fractal exploration. They extend beyond traditional fractals into oscillators, attractors, surfaces, knots, and experimental mathematical systems.

Oscillators and Fractal Maps

Naming of chaotic oscillators and fractal maps is difficult because even small changes in variables can produce entirely new structures.

Marcus introduced a specialised colouring scheme for fractal maps. To view raw colouring, disable “Use Special Colouring” in the Fractal Map selection dialog.

These systems may exist in 3 or 4 dimensions and can be visualised along multiple axes. Full 360° animation is possible where dimensionality permits.

Each oscillator or fractal map may be viewed in multiple coordinate systems: Cartesian, spherical, cylindrical, toroidal, and others — effectively multiplying the number of possible images.

Refer also to Comments on Oscillators, Knots and Surfaces.

Rotation and Axes

Two methods are used to determine the centre of rotation:

  • Viewing points within a defined volume
  • Using the statistical centre of mass

Axes are encoded using alphanumeric notation (1–9, A–Z for 10–35). Example: 4DH represents dimensions 4, 13, and 17.

Refer to Fractal Animation.

Selected Fractal Systems

ZigZag Spirals

Proto-fractal spirals inspired by Lauwerier: 

x = x0 * cos(a / k1) ± (y0 + x0^e1) * sin(a)
y = x0 * sin(a) ± (x + x0^e2) * cos(a)

Spirals act as building blocks of fractals and nature.

Cross Roads

An original fractal leading into structures reminiscent of Lyapunov, Volterra-Lotka, and others. 

x = y0 - (x0 + y0 / ABS(x0)) / (ABS(b * x0 + y0))
y = a + c - x

Explore freely — you may discover unexpected structures.
